Introduction

Zeroe is a climate tech company building intelligent data infrastructure for sustainability and impact. Our evolved brand identity differentiates us from competitors through distinctive gradient treatments, multi-color headline typography, and abstract 3D terrain imagery.

This document outlines the core elements of our visual identity system. Please refer to these guidelines when creating any Zeroe-branded materials to ensure consistency across all touchpoints.

Brand Voice & Tone

Our brand voice is the personality that comes through in all our communications. Write content that is:

Attribute Description
Informed & Factual Backed by data and expertise. We speak with authority because we know our subject.
Clever Smart without being condescending. We find elegant ways to explain complex topics.
Conversational Professional but approachable. We talk with people, not at them.
Concise Avoid dense, verbose, or overly technical language. Every word earns its place.

Typography

Our typography system uses two complementary font families: Inter for headings and Work Sans for body text.

Font Hierarchy

Level Font Weight Usage
H1 / Main Heading Inter Black (900) One per design, use gradient treatment
H2 / Subheading Inter Medium (500) Section headers, gradient or Zeroe Blue
Body / Paragraphs Work Sans Regular (400) All body copy, in Charcoal

Gradient Headline Treatment

This is the signature visual differentiator for Zeroe. Large headlines use a horizontal gradient that flows from Zeroe Blue through Muted Purple to Dusty Rose.

"Performance, capital, and compliance all live on clean data"

Gradient Specification:
Direction: Left to Right (90deg)
Start: #2673EA (Zeroe Blue)
Middle: #8B7B9E (Muted Purple)
End: #E07065 (Coral/Salmon)

Font Sources

Inter: fonts.google.com/specimen/Inter

Work Sans: fonts.google.com/specimen/Work+Sans

Logo Usage

Our logo is the most recognizable element of our brand. Use it consistently to build recognition and trust.

Logo Versions

Version When to Use
logo-blue Primary logo for light backgrounds
logo-black Used on light gradient backgrounds
logo-white For dark backgrounds

Logo Rules

  • Always leave adequate negative space around the logo
  • Logo appears in the top-right corner of presentations
  • On gradient backgrounds, use the black logo
  • Never distort, rotate, or modify logo colors

Signature Background Gradient

The warm gradient is the most distinctive visual element of the evolved brand. It creates human warmth in an enterprise product.

Direction: Bottom-left to top-right (135deg)

Colors:
• Light grey/white base (#F5F5F7 to #FFFFFF)
• Soft blue-lavender tint (top-left area)
• Warm peach/coral wash (bottom-right, #FDE8E4 to #F9D5CF)

Usage Rules

  • Apply to all presentation slides as base background
  • Apply to document pages (subtle version)
  • The gradient should feel "barely there" – soft and sophisticated
  • Gradient intensity: 5-15% opacity wash, never saturated

What NOT to Do

Do NOT use solid color backgrounds (except for tables/data sections)

Imagery Style

Our imagery creates a unique visual language that sets us apart from generic stock photography.

Primary: Abstract 3D Terrain

Our presentations feature distinctive 3D topographic/terrain visualizations: rendered mountain/landscape forms with charcoal/slate peaks transitioning to warm coral/peach/cream valleys. This abstract, data-visualization aesthetic creates a unique visual identity.

Secondary: Nature Photography

When using nature photography, prefer winter/snow landscapes (subtle, not vibrant green) with muted, desaturated color treatment. Use sparingly for closing slides or case studies.

What NOT to Use

  • Generic stock photography
  • Bright green "sustainability" clichés
  • Flat illustrations
  • Overly saturated nature imagery

Quick Reference

Creating Presentations

  1. Apply signature gradient to all slide backgrounds
  2. Place black logo in top-right corner
  3. Use gradient treatment for main headlines
  4. Section headers in Dusty Rose or Zeroe Blue
  5. Body text in charcoal Work Sans
  6. Use coral-colored progress indicators
  7. Include 3D terrain imagery on cover slides

Creating Documents

  1. Subtle gradient or white background
  2. Gradient headlines for chapter/section titles
  3. Zeroe Blue for subheadings
  4. Body text in Work Sans Regular, charcoal
  5. Tables with blue headers

Creating Data Visualizations

  1. Primary series in Zeroe Blue
  2. Secondary series from accent palette
  3. White chart background
  4. Clear legends in Work Sans
  5. Avoid more than 5 colors per chart

Creating Social Media Content

  1. Apply gradient background
  2. Bold gradient headline treatment
  3. Keep text minimal and punchy
  4. Include Zeroe logo
